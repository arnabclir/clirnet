Note: Modified Brooke uses 2 mL coefficient (vs 4 mL Parkland), reducing fluid overload risk. Adjust based on urine output 0.5-1.0 mL/kg/h for adults.
Formula:
Total 24h = 2 mL × TBSA (%) × Weight (kg)
Use Ringer's Lactate
First 8h = 50% of total
Last 16h = 50% of total

Advantages over Parkland:
- Reduces fluid creep
- Decreases hyperchloremic acidosis
- Lower complication rates
- Better outcomes in studies
