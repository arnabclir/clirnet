WHO BMI Classification

BMI Range (kg/m²) Classification Health Risk Clinical Intervention
< 18.5 Underweight Nutritional deficiency risk Assess for malnutrition, underlying causes
18.5 - 24.9 Normal Weight Minimal metabolic risk Maintain current weight
25.0 - 29.9 Overweight Increased cardiovascular risk Lifestyle modification counseling
30.0 - 34.9 Obese Class I Significant metabolic risk Weight management program
35.0 - 39.9 Obese Class II High metabolic risk Intensive intervention; consider pharmacotherapy
≥ 40.0 Obese Class III Very high metabolic risk Comprehensive evaluation; consider bariatric surgery

Ideal Body Weight Formulas

Devine Formula (Standard):

  • Males: 50 kg + 2.3 kg per inch above 5 feet
  • Females: 45.5 kg + 2.3 kg per inch above 5 feet

Miller Formula:

  • Males: 56.2 kg + 1.41 kg per cm above 152.4 cm
  • Females: 53.1 kg + 1.36 kg per cm above 152.4 cm

Robinson Formula:

  • Males: 52 kg + 1.9 kg per inch above 5 feet
  • Females: 49 kg + 1.7 kg per inch above 5 feet

Clinical Considerations

  • Muscle Mass: Athletes and muscular individuals may have high BMI without excess body fat
  • Age: BMI interpretation may differ in elderly populations
  • Ethnicity: Different ethnic groups may have varying metabolic risk at same BMI
  • Comorbidities: Consider diabetes, hypertension, and other conditions in risk assessment
  • Waist Circumference: Central obesity carries greater metabolic risk than peripheral obesity
  • Fitness Level: Cardiovascular fitness may modify health risks associated with BMI
