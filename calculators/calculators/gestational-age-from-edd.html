Gestational Age Reference
Gestational Age Categories: FIRST TRIMESTER (0-13 weeks): - Weeks 0-4: Conception and implantation - Weeks 5-8: Rapid organ development - Weeks 9-13: Fetal movements begin, CRL measurement - Dating: Clinical + ultrasound confirmation SECOND TRIMESTER (14-27 weeks): - Weeks 14-18: Quickening (mother feels movement) - Weeks 18-22: Anatomy scan/detailed ultrasound - Weeks 23-27: Fetal viability begins (with support) - Dating accuracy: ±1-2 weeks by ultrasound THIRD TRIMESTER (28-40+ weeks): - Weeks 28-36: Preterm risk decreases - Weeks 37-39: Early term/term - Weeks 40+: Post-term/overdue - Dating accuracy: ±3-4 weeks by ultrasound Clinical Milestones: - 6 weeks: Fetal heart rate visible on ultrasound - 12 weeks: Nuchal translucency screening - 20 weeks: Anatomy ultrasound, fetal sex determination - 24 weeks: Viability with neonatal intensive care - 35-37 weeks: Group B Strep testing - 40 weeks: Due date/term pregnancy - 42 weeks: Post-term, increased monitoring needed Monitoring Recommendations: - Preterm (<37 weeks): More frequent visits - Term (37-40 weeks): Standard monitoring - Post-term (>42 weeks): Increased surveillance - Abnormal growth: Ultrasound assessment
