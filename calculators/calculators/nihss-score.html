NIHSS Score

National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale (0-42 points)
Instructions: Complete neurological assessment and select appropriate score for each item
1a. Level of Consciousness (LOC)
1b. LOC Questions (Month and age)
1c. LOC Commands (Open/Close eyes, make fist)
2. Best Gaze (Horizontal eye movement)
3. Visual Fields (Confrontation)
4. Facial Palsy (Movement at rest and to effort)
5a. Motor Arm - Left (Drift, pronation)
5b. Motor Arm - Right (Drift, pronation)
6a. Motor Leg - Left (Drift)
6b. Motor Leg - Right (Drift)
7. Limb Ataxia (Finger-nose, heel-shin)
8. Sensory (Light touch, pin prick)
9. Best Language (Naming, comprehension, speech)
10. Dysarthria (Articulation, clarity)
11. Extinction/Inattention (Neglect)

NIHSS Score

-
Clinical Interpretation
  • 0: No stroke symptoms
  • 1-4: Minor stroke (low risk)
  • 5-15: Moderate stroke
  • 16-20: Moderate to severe stroke
  • 21-42: Severe stroke
Reference

Brott T, et al. Measurements of acute cerebral infarction: a clinical examination scale. Stroke. 1989;20(7):864-870.
