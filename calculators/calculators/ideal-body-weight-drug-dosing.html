Ideal Body Weight & Adjusted Body Weight
For Drug Dosing and Clinical Assessment
About This Calculator: Calculate ideal body weight (IBW), adjusted body weight (ABW), and BMI for appropriate drug dosing in obese patients and clinical assessment.
Formulas:
IBW (Males): 50 kg + 2.3 kg × (height in inches - 60)
IBW (Females): 45.5 kg + 2.3 kg × (height in inches - 60)
ABW: IBW + 0.4 × (Actual Body Weight - IBW)
BMI: Weight (kg) / Height (m)²

Clinical Notes
Ideal Body Weight (IBW): Theoretical weight for a given height assuming ideal body composition
Adjusted Body Weight (ABW): Used for dosing hydrophilic drugs in obese patients to account for increased volume of distribution
Use IBW: For most drugs, especially hydrophilic medications
Use ABW: For drugs that are lipophilic and distribute into fat (aminoglycosides, vancomycin)
Use Actual BW: For lipophilic drugs that distribute significantly into fat tissue
