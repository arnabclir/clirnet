GO-FAR Score

Good Outcome Following Attempted Resuscitation

Clinical Context:

GO-FAR predicts likelihood of favorable neurological outcome after out-of-hospital cardiac arrest (OHCA). Helps guide prognostication and shared decision-making.

Clinical Interpretation
Key Prognostic Factors:
  • Age: Younger age improves outcomes
  • Witnessed arrest: Better outcomes when witnessed
  • Bystander CPR: Improves neurological outcomes
  • Initial rhythm: VF/VT associated with better outcomes
  • Time to ROSC: Earlier ROSC improves survival
  • Post-resuscitation pH: Higher pH indicates better metabolic status

Clinical Note: GO-FAR assists with prognostication but should not be used in isolation. Neurological status, comorbidities, and patient/family values are essential in decision-making.
