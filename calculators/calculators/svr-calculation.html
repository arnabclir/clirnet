Systemic Vascular Resistance

SVR and SVRI Calculation from Hemodynamic Data

Clinical Context:

SVR measures systemic vascular function and afterload. Essential for hemodynamic assessment, cardiogenic shock evaluation, and guiding vasopressor/vasodilator therapy.

Normal: 70-100 mmHg
Normal: 2-8 mmHg
Normal: 4-8 L/min
Normal: ~1.8 m²

Formulas
Systemic Vascular Resistance (SVR):

SVR = (MAP - CVP) / CO × 80
[Units: mmHg·min/L]

SVR Index (SVRI):

SVRI = SVR × BSA

Clinical Note: Low SVR suggests vasodilation (sepsis). High SVR suggests vasoconstriction. Use with CO to determine shock type and guide therapy.
