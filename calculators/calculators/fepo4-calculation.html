Fractional Excretion of Phosphate

FePO4 Assessment for Phosphate Metabolism

Clinical Context:

FePO4 helps assess phosphate reabsorption by the kidneys. Useful in evaluating hypophosphatemia and disorders of phosphate metabolism.

Normal: 2.5-4.5 mg/dL
Normal: 0.6-1.2 mg/dL

FePO4 Interpretation
FePO4 Range Interpretation Mechanism
<5%LowHigh phosphate reabsorption
5-10%NormalAppropriate phosphate handling
>10%HighReduced phosphate reabsorption
Formula
Fractional Excretion of Phosphate:

FePO4 = (Urine PO4 × Serum Cr / Serum PO4 × Urine Cr) × 100

Clinical Applications:
  • Low FePO4 (<5%): High PTH activity, primary hyperparathyroidism
  • High FePO4 (>10%): Secondary hyperparathyroidism, FGF23-mediated, hypophosphatemic rickets
  • Helps differentiate causes of hypophosphatemia
  • Use with PTH level for complete evaluation
