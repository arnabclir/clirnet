Calcium Correction - Conventional Units

Adjusted Serum Calcium (mg/dL) - Hypoalbuminemia Correction

Clinical Use: Corrects total serum calcium for low albumin levels (conventional units)
Laboratory Values
mg/dL
Normal range: 8.8-10.4 mg/dL
g/dL
Normal range: 3.5-5.5 g/dL
Formula & Interpretation
Corrected Ca = Measured Ca + 0.8 × (4 - Albumin)
Where albumin is in g/dL and calcium in mg/dL
Normal Reference Range
Corrected Calcium: 8.8 - 10.4 mg/dL
Clinical Interpretation

Hypercalcemia: Corrected Ca > 10.4 mg/dL

Hypocalcemia: Corrected Ca < 8.8 mg/dL

Normal: Corrected Ca 8.8 - 10.4 mg/dL

When to Use This Calculator
  • Patient has low serum albumin (<4.0 g/dL)
  • Need accurate assessment of calcium status
  • Total calcium may be falsely low due to hypoalbuminemia
  • Using conventional units (mg/dL)
Unit Conversions:
Calcium: 1 mmol/L = 4 mg/dL
Albumin: 1 g/L = 0.1 g/dL
Clinical Notes

Approximately 45% of serum calcium is bound to albumin, while the remaining is either ionized or complexed. When serum albumin is low, total serum calcium appears falsely decreased.

Key Points:
  • Use corrected calcium to interpret total calcium levels in hypoalbuminemia
  • Ionized calcium is more accurate but less commonly measured
  • This formula is most commonly used in North America (conventional units)
  • Correction is not needed if ionized calcium is available
  • Consider phosphate and vitamin D levels in interpretation
Common Causes of Hypoalbuminemia:
  • Liver disease (cirrhosis, hepatitis)
  • Nephrotic syndrome
  • Malnutrition
  • Protein-losing enteropathy
  • Sepsis or systemic illness
Note: This is one of several correction formulas available. Verify institutional protocols before clinical use.
