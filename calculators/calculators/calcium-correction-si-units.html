Calcium Correction - SI Units

Adjusted Serum Calcium (mmol/L) - Hypoalbuminemia Correction

Clinical Use: Corrects total serum calcium for low albumin levels (SI units)
Laboratory Values
mmol/L
Normal range: 2.2-2.6 mmol/L
g/L
Normal range: 35-55 g/L
Formula & Interpretation
Corrected Ca = Measured Ca + 0.02 × (40 - Albumin)
Where albumin is in g/L and calcium in mmol/L
Normal Reference Range
Corrected Calcium: 2.2 - 2.6 mmol/L
Clinical Interpretation

Hypercalcemia: Corrected Ca > 2.6 mmol/L

Hypocalcemia: Corrected Ca < 2.2 mmol/L

Normal: Corrected Ca 2.2 - 2.6 mmol/L

When to Use This Calculator
  • Patient has low serum albumin (<40 g/L)
  • Need accurate assessment of calcium status
  • Total calcium may be falsely low due to hypoalbuminemia
  • Using SI units (mmol/L)
Clinical Notes

Approximately 45% of serum calcium is bound to albumin, while the remaining is either ionized or complexed. When serum albumin is low, total serum calcium appears falsely decreased.

Key Points:
  • Use corrected calcium to interpret total calcium levels in hypoalbuminemia
  • Ionized calcium is more accurate but less commonly measured
  • Different correction factors may be used in different institutions
  • Correction is not needed if ionized calcium is available
  • Consider phosphate and vitamin D levels in interpretation
Note: This is one of several correction formulas available. Verify institutional protocols before clinical use.
