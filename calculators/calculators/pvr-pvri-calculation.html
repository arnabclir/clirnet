Pulmonary Vascular Resistance

PVR and PVRI Calculation from Hemodynamic Data

Clinical Context:

PVR measures pulmonary vascular function. Essential for diagnosing pulmonary hypertension and assessing right ventricular function during catheterization.

Normal: 12-16 mmHg
Also called PCWP; Normal: 2-12 mmHg
Normal: 4-8 L/min
Normal: ~1.8 m²

PVR:

mmHg/L/min

PVRI:

mmHg/L/min/m²

Reference Values
Parameter Normal Range Abnormal
PVR<2 mmHg/L/min>3 mmHg/L/min

mPAP12-16 mmHg>25 mmHg (PH)
Formulas
Pulmonary Vascular Resistance (PVR):

PVR = (mPAP - PAWP) / CO

PVR Index (PVRI):

PVRI = PVR × BSA

Clinical Note: Elevated PVR indicates pulmonary hypertension. PVR >3 mmHg/L/min or PVRI >5 mmHg/L/min/m² suggest PH and may affect treatment decisions.
