Cardiac Output Calculation

Hemodynamic assessment using Fick method and others

Clinical Context:

Cardiac output is the volume of blood pumped by the heart per minute. Essential for hemodynamic assessment and diagnosis of cardiogenic shock.

Normal: 200-250 mL/min (rest)
Normal: 19-20 vol%
Normal: 14-15 vol%
Normal: 60-100 bpm
Normal: 70 mL

Formulas
Fick Method:

CO = VO₂ / (CaO₂ - CvO₂) × 10

Heart Rate × Stroke Volume Method:

CO = HR × SV / 1000

Cardiac Index (BSA Method):

CI = CO / BSA

Clinical Note: Low CO (<4 L/min) may indicate cardiogenic shock or need for inotropic support. Always assess in context of clinical presentation.
