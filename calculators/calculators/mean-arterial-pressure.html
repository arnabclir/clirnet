Mean Arterial Pressure

MAP Calculation from Systolic and Diastolic BP

Clinical Context:

MAP represents the average pressure in arteries during one cardiac cycle. Essential for hemodynamic assessment and shock evaluation. Critical threshold: MAP ≥65 mmHg for organ perfusion.

Formulas
Mean Arterial Pressure (Standard):

MAP = DBP + 1/3(SBP - DBP)
OR
MAP = (SBP + 2×DBP) / 3

Pulse Pressure:

PP = SBP - DBP

Clinical Notes:

  • MAP <65 mmHg: Risk of inadequate organ perfusion
  • MAP >100 mmHg: Risk of end-organ damage
  • Pulse pressure >60 mmHg: Vascular stiffness, increased CV risk
  • Essential for shock assessment and vasopressor titration
