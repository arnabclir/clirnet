Sodium Correction for Hyperglycemia

Corrected Plasma Sodium Concentration

Clinical Tool: Corrects measured sodium for high glucose levels
Laboratory Values
mEq/L
Normal range: 135-145 mEq/L
mg/dL
Correction needed if >200 mg/dL
Formula & Explanation
Corrected Na = Measured Na + 0.016 × (Glucose - 100)
Where glucose > 100 mg/dL
Normal Reference Range

Plasma Sodium: 135 - 145 mEq/L

Why Correction Matters

High blood glucose draws water out of cells (osmotic effect), diluting the serum sodium concentration. The measured sodium appears falsely low.

The corrected sodium provides a more accurate assessment of true sodium status.

Clinical Interpretation:

Hyponatremia: Corrected Na < 135 mEq/L

Normal: Corrected Na 135 - 145 mEq/L

Hypernatremia: Corrected Na > 145 mEq/L

Clinical Notes
When to Use This Calculator
  • Plasma glucose > 200 mg/dL present
  • Concerned about true sodium status
  • Patient has hyperglycemia and hyponatremia
  • Managing DKA or HHS (hyperglycemic hyperosmolar state)
Important: For every 100 mg/dL increase in glucose above 100 mg/dL, sodium decreases by approximately 2.4 mEq/L due to osmotic effects.
Key Clinical Points:
  • Pseudohyponatremia occurs with hyperglycemia
  • After normalizing glucose, sodium will rise
  • Monitor sodium carefully during insulin therapy
  • Watch for hyponatremia signs (confusion, seizures, coma)
  • Free water deficit may also contribute to hypernatremia after correction
